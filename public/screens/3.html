Understanding Tarot:
A Journey Through Symbolism

Wisdom of Ages

Tarot has a rich history spanning more than 500 years. Originally created in 15th-century Italy as a card game, Tarot gradually evolved into a symbolic system used for guidance, reflection, and personal insight.

During the 18th and 19th centuries, scholars and mystics began exploring the deeper meanings behind the imagery, connecting the cards with philosophy, spirituality, mythology, and human experience. Today, Tarot is used worldwide as a tool for self-exploration rather than simply predicting the future.

The Modern Deck

Exploring the 78 keys to the human experience.

Modern Tarot consists of 78 cards divided into the Major Arcana and Minor Arcana. Each card represents themes, experiences, emotions, challenges, and opportunities that people commonly encounter throughout life.

Accessibility & Growth

In recent years, Tarot has become more accessible than ever through books, online communities, mobile apps, and digital readings.

Its enduring popularity lies in its ability to combine storytelling, symbolism, psychology, and intuition in a way that feels both personal and meaningful. Whether used for daily inspiration, self-development, or spiritual growth, Tarot offers a unique framework for uncovering deeper insights.
